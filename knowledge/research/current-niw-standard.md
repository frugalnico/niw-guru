# Current EB-2 NIW Standard — Research Note

**Baseline as of:** 2025–2026. Re-verify against USCIS before relying on it; immigration policy changes.

This note records the *current* USCIS NIW standard so the agent does not have to re-derive it at query time. Pair it with `knowledge/overview-niw.md` and `knowledge/prongs/`.

## The framework: Matter of Dhanasar (2016)
NIW eligibility is governed by the three-prong test from *Matter of Dhanasar*, 26 I&N Dec. 884 (AAO 2016). After establishing EB-2 eligibility (advanced degree or exceptional ability), the petitioner must show:

1. **Prong 1 — Substantial merit and national importance** of the proposed endeavor.
2. **Prong 2 — Well positioned** to advance the proposed endeavor.
3. **Prong 3 — On balance, beneficial** to waive the job-offer / labor-certification requirement.

## The January 15, 2025 policy update (PA-2025-03) — the big change
USCIS issued its most detailed NIW guidance in nearly a decade on **2025-01-15**, added to **Policy Manual Volume 6, Part F, Chapter 5**. It applies to every NIW petition pending on or filed after that date. It does **not** change the Dhanasar prongs — it clarifies how officers apply them:

- **"Proposed endeavor" must be specific**, not a general occupation. Describe *what* the work is and *how/where* it will be carried out. (This is exactly what the `future_endeavors.md` deliverable is built to provide.)
- **National importance** is about the endeavor's *broader implications* (national/global), not the prestige of the employer or the petitioner's job duties. Narrow geographic benefit is weaker; broad implications for a field, the economy, public health, or national security are stronger.
- **Support letters and business/research plans** are weighed for *specificity and corroboration*. Generic, conclusory letters carry little weight; detailed, evidence-backed ones carry much more.
- **Well-positioned (Prong 2)** is evaluated on track record + a realistic plan, not guaranteed success.

## Critical & Emerging Technologies (CET) — a strong positive factor
The 2025 guidance gives **special, favorable consideration** to work in **critical and emerging technologies** and national-security-related fields. The CET list (White House / NSTC) includes, among others:

- Quantum Information Technologies (sensing, computing, communication)
- Semiconductors & Microelectronics
- Advanced Computing / High-Performance Computing
- Artificial Intelligence & Machine Learning
- Advanced Communications / Networking
- Biotechnologies
- Advanced Energy & Clean Energy generation/storage
- Advanced Materials / Manufacturing
- Space Technologies, Hypersonics, Directed Energy
- Human-Machine Interfaces, Data Privacy/Security

Two consequences the agent should exploit when the petitioner's field maps onto a CET:
- A petitioner with an **advanced STEM degree (especially a PhD)** engaged in a CET and well positioned to advance it is a **"strong positive factor"** under **Prong 3**.
- Work in CETs **routinely satisfies Prong 1** (national importance).

**How the agent uses this:** During the *profile* stage, classify the petitioner's field against the CET list. If it maps, frame the proposed endeavor in CET language and the harvested federal evidence around the matching CET category and the U.S. competitiveness / national-security narrative. If it does *not* map to a CET, fall back to the general "broader implications" test — national problem → endeavor addresses it → government evidence shows the U.S. prioritizes it.

## Evidence implications (how this steers the pipeline)
- Frame the **proposed endeavor** in CET / national-priority language and tie it to named U.S. initiatives (e.g., CHIPS and Science Act, National Quantum Initiative, Cancer Moonshot, Bioeconomy EO, IRA clean-energy programs — whichever fits the field).
- The national-importance record should consist of **authoritative, quotable, downloadable** government sources (statutes, executive orders, agency strategies, funding solicitations) plus reputable media/think-tank coverage — this is the `niw-federal-evidence-harvester` skill's job.
- Recommendation letters must be **specific and corroborated** — independent (arms-length) experts carry more weight than close collaborators; both should cite concrete contributions and impact.
- Build the record so **every factual claim maps to a real source** — a file the user supplied or a verified web source. Zero unverified claims.
