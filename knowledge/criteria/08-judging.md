# Criterion 8: Judging the Work of Others

## Legal Standard
Evidence of the beneficiary's participation, on a panel or individually, as a judge of the work of others in the same or allied field. Two sub-elements: (1) participation as a judge (actually evaluated others' work), (2) in the same or allied field.

## Evidence Hierarchy (strongest to weakest)
1. Formal judge confirmation letter on event organizer's letterhead
2. Invitation email from organizer specifically inviting the beneficiary to judge
3. Completed score sheets / judging rubrics showing actual scoring
4. Public listing as a judge on event website (screenshot)
5. Photos at the event in judging capacity (corroboration only)

## Event Strength Factors

| Factor | Strong | Weak |
|--------|--------|------|
| Scale | 1,000+ participants, significant prizes | < 50 participants, no prizes |
| Selection to judge | Formal invitation based on expertise | Self-applied, anyone could judge |
| Scope | International, multiple countries | Single campus or classroom |
| Evidence | Letterhead confirmation + score sheets | Screenshot of a Slack message |
| Relevance | Directly in the beneficiary's field | Unrelated field |

## Argument Structure: Lead with Quality, Stack with Volume

### Tier 1 (2-3 best events): Full argument paragraphs
For each: event name, date, organizer, number of participants, acceptance rate, prize amount, countries represented, evidence of actual judging, significance in the field.

### Tier 2 (remaining events): Summary table
| Event | Date | Evidence |
|-------|------|----------|

### Closing paragraph
"In total, the Beneficiary served as a judge at [N]+ events between [date range], evaluating the work of others in [field]."

Tier 1 proves quality. Tier 2 proves volume and sustained pattern. Together they satisfy both Step 1 and Step 2 of Kazarian.

## Mentoring and Speaking
- **Accelerator mentoring** can qualify if framed as evaluative — mentoring founders involves evaluating their work and providing critical feedback. Be explicit about the evaluative nature since "mentoring" is not "judging."
- **Conference speaking** is weaker — speaking does not equal evaluating others' work. Use as corroboration, not primary evidence.

## Best Practices
- Include score sheets or completed rubrics to prove actual judging, not just an invitation
- Diversify event sources — university hackathons + business awards + accelerator mentoring + conferences (all from same organizer looks like one relationship)
- Include events where the beneficiary was invited/recruited, not just self-applied roles
- Every event must relate to the beneficiary's field — explain the connection if not obvious
- Lead with the most prestigious events and use smaller ones as volume evidence
- For each top event, include sourced statistics: participant count, prize amount, countries represented
