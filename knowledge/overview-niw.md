# EB-2 NIW: National Interest Waiver

## Legal Standard
- **Statute:** INA § 203(b)(2)
- **Regulation:** 8 CFR § 204.5(k)
- **Framework:** *Matter of Dhanasar*, 26 I&N Dec. 884 (AAO 2016)

Waives the job offer and labor certification requirements. The beneficiary must show it would be beneficial to the U.S. to grant the waiver.

## The Three Dhanasar Prongs (all must be met)
1. The proposed endeavor has both substantial merit and national importance
2. The beneficiary is well positioned to advance the proposed endeavor
3. On balance, it would be beneficial to the U.S. to waive the requirements

## Key Difference from O-1A/EB-1A
- O-1A/EB-1A = backward-looking: what have you done?
- NIW = forward-looking: what will you do, and why does the U.S. need you to do it?

## Who Files NIW
NIW is used across many fields — not just tech entrepreneurs:

| Field | Common Endeavors |
|-------|-----------------|
| STEM researchers | Advancing cancer research, developing new materials, climate science |
| Physicians/healthcare | Serving underserved communities, advancing medical specialties |
| Engineers | Infrastructure, clean energy, semiconductor manufacturing |
| Tech entrepreneurs | Building products that address national problems |
| Data scientists / AI | Advancing AI capabilities, cybersecurity, national defense applications |
| Educators | Developing curriculum, STEM education initiatives |
| Business professionals | Economic development, trade, workforce development |
| Artists / cultural | Preserving cultural heritage, advancing arts education (less common) |

## Letter Structure

1. **Opening** — Petition type, role, proposed endeavor (1 paragraph)
2. **Background** — Education, current position, prior experience (brief)
3. **The endeavor** — What the work is, what has been accomplished, traction
4. **Future plans** — Specific roadmap, R&D, partnerships, expansion
5. **National importance** — The U.S. problem + how the endeavor addresses it (longest section)
6. **Well positioned** — Track record, qualifications, resources
7. **Balance** — Why waiving job offer requirement benefits the U.S.
8. **Conclusion** — Summary + request

## Best Practices
- Letter is FIRST PERSON ("I" not "the Beneficiary") — this is a self-petition
- Every statistic must be sourced (government data preferred: CDC, NIH, BLS, NSF, DOE, EPA, etc.)
- Future plans must be specific and credible — not aspirational
- National importance section is often the longest — it builds the entire case
- The endeavor can be commercial, academic, nonprofit, or government-related — USCIS does not require any specific type
- Adapt the argument structure to the field — a researcher emphasizes publications and citations; a physician emphasizes patient outcomes and underserved areas; an entrepreneur emphasizes products and market impact
- For researchers: cite publication record, h-index, citation count, grant funding, lab resources
- For physicians: cite HPSA designations, patient volume, health outcome data for the region
- For entrepreneurs: cite product traction, funding, user adoption, economic impact
- For engineers: cite project scale, infrastructure impact, patents, industry adoption
