# Prong 1: Substantial Merit and National Importance

## Legal Standard
The proposed endeavor must have both substantial merit AND national importance.

**Substantial merit** = the endeavor has inherent value in its field
**National importance** = implications extend beyond a specific locality or employer

## Evidence Hierarchy
1. Government data on the national problem (CDC, NIH, BLS, NSF, DOE, EPA, DOD, USDA)
2. Government policy statements, executive orders, and funding initiatives
3. Published research quantifying the problem's scope and economic impact
4. Evidence of the endeavor's existing traction and geographic reach
5. Expert letters explaining the national significance

## Argument Structure

### 1. Define the endeavor specifically
Tailor to the field:
- **Researcher:** "Developing novel nanomaterial coatings for lithium-ion batteries to improve energy density and safety"
- **Physician:** "Providing primary care to medically underserved communities in rural Appalachia"
- **Engineer:** "Designing seismic-resilient bridge infrastructure for U.S. highway systems"
- **Entrepreneur:** "Building AI-powered diagnostic tools that reduce medical imaging interpretation time"
- **Educator:** "Developing STEM curriculum frameworks adopted by public school districts nationwide"

### 2. Establish the U.S. problem with data
- Cite 3-5 statistics from authoritative government sources
- Show scale (how many people/systems affected), cost (economic impact), and trajectory (worsening trend)
- Use the source most relevant to the field:

| Field | Key Government Sources |
|-------|----------------------|
| Healthcare / mental health | CDC, NIH, NIMH, SAMHSA, HRSA, CMS |
| Energy / environment | DOE, EPA, EIA |
| Technology / AI | NSF, NIST, OSTP, Commerce Dept |
| Infrastructure / engineering | DOT, FHWA, ASCE, FEMA |
| Education | Dept. of Education, NSF (STEM) |
| National security | DOD, DHS, CISA |
| Agriculture / food | USDA, FDA |
| Economics / workforce | BLS, DOL, Census Bureau |

### 3. Connect the endeavor to the problem
- Explain the specific mechanism: how does this work reduce the problem?
- Be concrete with numbers where possible
- Show scalability: how can this impact more people/systems over time?

### 4. Connect to national priorities
- Cite relevant government initiatives, executive orders, or federal funding programs
- Show alignment between the endeavor and stated U.S. policy goals
- Examples: CHIPS Act (semiconductors), Cancer Moonshot (oncology), AI Executive Order (AI), Infrastructure Act (engineering), HPSA designations (physicians)

## Best Practices
- National importance does NOT require benefiting the entire nation — implications beyond one locality suffice
- Government sources carry more weight than private industry reports
- The endeavor can be commercial, academic, clinical, or nonprofit
- Vague claims ("improving lives," "advancing science") are insufficient — specify what, how, and for whom
- The problem section builds the foundation — invest in making it data-rich
- For STEM researchers: connect to federal grant priorities (NSF, NIH, DOE research agendas)
- For physicians: cite HPSA/MUA designations for the service area + provider shortage statistics
- For engineers: cite infrastructure condition ratings (ASCE Report Card), safety statistics, federal investment
- For entrepreneurs: cite the market gap, affected population size, inadequacy of current solutions
