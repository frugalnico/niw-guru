# Key USCIS Policy Alerts and Precedent Decisions

## Policy Alerts

### PA-2022-03 (January 21, 2022) — Equity as High Remuneration
**Critical for:** O-1A Criterion 7, EB-1A Criterion 8

**Key quote:**
> "As another example, if the petitioner demonstrates that receipt of a high salary is not readily applicable to the beneficiary's position as an entrepreneur, the petitioner might present evidence that the beneficiary's highly valued equity holdings in the startup are of comparable significance to the high salary criterion."

**How to use:** Cite this whenever the beneficiary is a startup founder with low salary but significant equity. Always include the full policy alert as an exhibit.

**Applies to:** Both O-1A and EB-1A high remuneration arguments.

## Precedent Decisions

### Matter of Kazarian, 596 F.3d 1115 (9th Cir. 2010)
**Critical for:** All O-1A and EB-1A petitions

**What it established:** The two-step analytical framework:
1. **Step 1:** Does the evidence satisfy the plain language of at least 3 (O-1) or 3 (EB-1A) criteria?
2. **Step 2:** Considering the totality of evidence, does the beneficiary demonstrate sustained national or international acclaim?

**How to use:** Structure the petition to clearly address both steps. Even after meeting 3 criteria at Step 1, include a "totality" argument showing the overall pattern of extraordinary ability.

### Matter of Dhanasar, 26 I&N Dec. 884 (AAO 2016)
**Critical for:** EB-2 NIW petitions

**What it established:** The three-prong test for National Interest Waiver:
1. The proposed endeavor has both substantial merit and national importance
2. The beneficiary is well positioned to advance the proposed endeavor
3. On balance, it would be beneficial to the United States to waive the job offer and labor certification requirements

**Replaced:** Matter of New York State Department of Transportation (NYSDOT)

## Useful AAO Non-Precedent Decisions

These are not binding but show how adjudicators think:

- **VC funding as awards:** Multiple AAO decisions have upheld VC funding as qualifying under the awards criterion when properly framed with selection criteria and expert testimony
- **Accelerator membership as association:** AAO has accepted selective accelerator programs (YC, Techstars, etc.) as qualifying associations when selectivity is documented
- **Online platform awards (Product Hunt, etc.):** Weaker standalone; AAO has been mixed. Best used as corroboration, not primary evidence for the awards criterion
