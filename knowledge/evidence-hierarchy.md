# Universal Evidence Hierarchy

How USCIS weighs evidence, from strongest to weakest. This applies across all petition types.

## Tier 1: Independent Third-Party Evidence
Evidence from sources with no relationship to the beneficiary. Hardest to fabricate, most trusted by adjudicators.

| Type | Example | Why It's Strong |
|------|---------|----------------|
| Government/institutional records | BLS data, patent grants, court records | Official, verifiable, impossible to fake |
| Major media coverage | Forbes article, NYT profile, BBC coverage | Editorial gatekeeping; journalist independently verified |
| Peer-reviewed publications | Journal articles with DOI, citation counts | Expert review process; independently verifiable |
| Award certificates from established orgs | Stevie Award, Asia Design Prize | Org has no incentive to fabricate |

## Tier 2: Solicited Third-Party Evidence
Evidence from independent sources that was requested for the petition. Still third-party, but the source knows it's for immigration.

| Type | Example | Why It's Still Good |
|------|---------|-------------------|
| Expert testimonial letters (from strangers) | Letter from a professor or industry leader who doesn't know the beneficiary personally | Independent assessment, even though solicited |
| Organization support letters to USCIS | Forbes Business Council visa letter with acceptance rate | Org has reputation at stake; specific to immigration |
| Employer verification letters (from HR) | HR letter confirming title, duties, dates | Official company record, signed by HR |

## Tier 3: Related-Party Evidence
Evidence from people/organizations connected to the beneficiary. Adjudicators give this less weight.

| Type | Example | Limitation |
|------|---------|-----------|
| Expert letters from colleagues/co-founders | CTO writes letter for CEO's petition | "Of course your co-founder says nice things" |
| Company pitch decks | Beneficiary's own startup deck | Self-serving marketing material |
| Self-reported metrics | "50K monthly users" without third-party verification | Could be inflated |

## Tier 4: Self-Generated Evidence
Weakest. Only useful as corroboration, never as primary evidence.

| Type | Example | Limitation |
|------|---------|-----------|
| Resume/CV | Lists accomplishments | Self-reported, unverified |
| Screenshots of own products | App UI, code snippets | Proves existence, not significance |
| Social media posts | Twitter reviews, LinkedIn endorsements | Informal, unvetted |

## Practical Application

For every criterion, try to have at least one piece of Tier 1 or Tier 2 evidence. If the strongest evidence for a criterion is Tier 3, the argument is vulnerable to RFE.

**The ideal evidence stack per criterion:**
1. One Tier 1 piece (press coverage, award from established org, published data)
2. One Tier 2 piece (expert letter, org support letter)
3. Multiple Tier 3/4 pieces as corroboration (pitch deck, screenshots, metrics)
