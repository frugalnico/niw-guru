## I. The petitioner and the proposed endeavor

Dr. A. Researcher works in long-duration grid-scale energy storage, with demonstrated command of
earth-abundant electrode chemistry (iron-air, sodium-ion), operando characterization of
deep-cycling electrodes (XRD/Raman), and ML-guided materials screening (sample pubs 1–4). The
work is aimed squarely at cheaper, domestically sourced storage that can hold energy for 10+ hours.

**Proposed endeavor:** *Earth-abundant 10+ hour iron-air cells for the U.S. grid* — develop
low-cost, domestically sourced iron-air storage that meets DOE's long-duration cost/duration
targets.

## II. The endeavor has substantial merit and national importance (Prong 1)

Grid-scale storage is a formally designated Critical & Emerging Technology, and the specific
cost/duration problem this endeavor targets is a named federal goal:

> "[illustrative] target a 90% cost reduction for grid-scale storage that delivers 10+ hours of
> duration within the decade" — DOE "Long Duration Storage Shot", `02_DOE_Long_Duration_Storage_Shot.pdf`, PDF p. N  `[VERIFY]`

> "[illustrative] Renewable energy generation and storage … advanced energy technologies" — Critical and Emerging Technologies List Update, `01_CET_List_2024.pdf`, PDF p. N  `[VERIFY]`

Advanced energy storage sits within the CET list, and under the Jan-2025 USCIS guidance an
advanced-STEM PhD advancing a CET is a strong positive factor — so the field readily satisfies
substantial merit and national importance.

## III. The petitioner is well positioned to advance the endeavor (Prong 2)

The endeavor's plan — additive-stabilized iron electrodes validated with operando XRD, then a
pouch-cell prototype benchmarked against the DOE target — sits directly on the petitioner's prior
work on earth-abundant electrodes and operando characterization (sample pubs 1, 3), with
ML-screening experience (sample pub 2) accelerating additive selection.
