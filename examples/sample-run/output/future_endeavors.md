## Endeavor 1 — Earth-abundant 10+ hour iron-air cells for the U.S. grid
**Thesis:** Develop low-cost, domestically sourced iron-air storage that meets DOE's
long-duration cost/duration targets.

**Objective.** A cell chemistry and electrode architecture delivering 10+ hour discharge at a
fraction of today's $/kWh, using only earth-abundant, U.S.-available materials.

**Technical approach.** Iterate iron-air electrode formulations; suppress parasitic reactions;
validate with operando XRD/Raman; use ML screening to prioritize additives.

**Sub-aims.**
- *Aim 1 —* Stabilize the iron electrode against capacity fade. *Approach:* additive screening
  + operando XRD. *Builds on:* sample pubs 1, 3.
- *Aim 2 —* Scale a pouch-cell prototype and benchmark cost/duration vs. the DOE target.

**Milestones & timeline.**
- *Years 1–2:* additive-stabilized electrode; coin-cell 10-hour demonstration.
- *Years 3–4:* pouch-cell prototype; independent cost model.
- *Years 5+:* pilot-line transfer with a U.S. manufacturer.

**Why the petitioner is well positioned (Prong 2).** Direct prior work on earth-abundant
electrodes and operando characterization (sample pubs 1, 3).

**National-interest alignment (Prong 1).** Maps to the DOE Long-Duration Storage Shot cost/
duration goals and the CET designation of advanced energy storage (see
`national_importance_quotes.md`, blocks 1–2). *(Illustrative; verify sources in a real run.)*

**Feasibility, risk & impact.** Risk: cycle-life at scale. Impact: cheaper grid storage enables
higher renewable penetration and grid reliability.
